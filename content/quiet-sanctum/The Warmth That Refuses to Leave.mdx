There are those in Verasanth who do not carry fire.

They carry what remains after it.

The Hearthborn do not burn brightly. They do not consume. Their presence is something quieter — the kind of warmth that lingers in stone long after the flame has been taken away.

It is not enough to drive back the dark.

But it is enough to endure it.

Their work is not victory. It is continuation.

A steady hand. A slowed breath. The moment when pain does not worsen, and that alone becomes a kind of mercy.

The Sanctum teaches that this is strength.

That to restore what is nearly lost is an act of defiance.

Some who pass through its halls believe them.

Others leave quickly.

The city does not favor what refuses to break.

But neither does it know how to extinguish it.

And so the warmth remains.

Not loud. Not triumphant.

Just… present.
