People come to the Sanctum for comfort.

They expect warmth.
Soft light.
A place where the city feels… quieter.

For a while, that’s what they get.

The flame is steady.
The air is still.
Voices drop without being asked.

It feels like safety.

Halden never says it is.

He talks about direction instead.
About holding something steady in a place that prefers movement.

Hope, he calls it.

Not a shield. Not protection.

Just… a way to keep going.

Most people don’t stay long enough to notice the other part.

The flame reacts.

Not dramatically. Not in ways you can prove.

But it shifts.

A little brighter when certain thoughts pass through your mind.
A little dimmer when something settles in your chest that you don’t want to name.

It listens.

And if you sit there long enough—quiet enough—you’ll start to feel it listening back.

Not judging. Not deciding.

Just… aware.

The first time it happens, people usually stand up.

They thank Halden.
They leave.
They tell themselves they imagined it.

He doesn’t stop them.

But if you ever look back before stepping out into the street—

you might notice the flame leaning toward you.

Not reaching.

Just… acknowledging.
