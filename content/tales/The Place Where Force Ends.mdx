There are those in Verasanth who step aside.

The Wardens do not.

They choose their ground, and once chosen, they remain — not because they cannot move, but because something has to be there when the moment arrives.

The blow comes.

It always does.

Not all at once, and not always from where it is expected, but with certainty enough that the city itself seems to lean into it. Pressure. Intent. The quiet understanding that something must give way.

The Warden answers this with stillness.

Not resistance in the sense of force against force, but something heavier. A presence that draws attention, that gathers the danger and holds it long enough for others to survive what would otherwise reach them.

They do not deflect what comes.

They take it.

And in taking it, they decide where it ends.

There is a point, in every encounter, where the body should fail. Where strength should falter and the line should break.

The Wardens are taught to recognize that moment.

And to remain anyway.

The Watch calls this duty.

Others call it sacrifice.

The city has no word for it.

It only knows that, for a time…

the pressure stops there.
