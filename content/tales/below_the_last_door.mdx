There’s always another door.

That’s what people tell themselves.

That whatever they’ve seen so far — the sewers, the tunnels, the things that don’t quite fit — is just another layer.

Another level. Another challenge.

Something that can be mapped.

Measured.

Beaten.

They’re wrong.

At a certain depth, the doors stop behaving like doors.

They don’t lead somewhere.

They separate states.

Above them, the city makes sense.

Rules hold.
Patterns repeat.
Even the strange parts feel… contained.

Below them—

things stop pretending.

You’ll know when you reach one.

Not because of how it looks.
It won’t be marked.

You’ll know because of how everything else changes.

Sound dulls.
Air thickens.
The sense of direction… loosens.

And the door itself—

it won’t feel like something you open.

It will feel like something that’s been waiting for you to arrive.

Most people hesitate.

Some turn back.

A few go through.

The ones who do don’t come back the same.

Not broken.

Not lost.

Just… aligned differently.

And if you ever find yourself standing in front of a door that doesn’t look like it leads anywhere—

take a moment.

Because once you pass it,
you won’t be asking where you are anymore.

You’ll be asking what you’ve become part of.
