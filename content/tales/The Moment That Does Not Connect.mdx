There are moments in Verasanth that do not connect.

An action taken.

A result observed.

And nothing between them.

The Shadowbound live there.

They do not vanish in the way others understand it. They do not become invisible, nor do they move with impossible speed. What they do is step outside the thread that binds one moment to the next.

For a time, they are not part of what is happening.

The world continues.

They do not.

When they return, it is not an arrival.

It is a correction.

The space they left collapses, and something is suddenly where it was not before. A strike lands without warning. A presence resolves where there was only uncertainty.

Those who witness it struggle to describe what occurred.

They remember the outcome.

They cannot recall the cause.

The Covenant teaches that this is not a trick of perception.

It is something more fundamental.

That time, like shadow, does not always behave as expected.

And that there are places within it—

brief, unstable, and easily lost—

where a person can step aside…

and decide what the next moment will be.
