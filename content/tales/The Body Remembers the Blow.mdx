There are those in Verasanth who do not train for battle.

They arrive already shaped by it.

The Ironblood do not speak of strength. They speak of impact — the moment something meets resistance and decides whether it will continue.

Their strikes are not wild. They are deliberate. Measured. A single motion carried through to its end without hesitation.

When they strike, it is not the force that matters.

It is what follows.

The body shifts. Balance falters. The world stutters for a breath.

That is enough.

The Banner teaches that control is the difference between survival and noise.

The Ironblood do not agree.

To them, survival was never a lesson.

It was the only option that remained.
