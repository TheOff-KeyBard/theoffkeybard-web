People think fire ends when it goes dark.

That once the flame is gone, the danger is over.

That’s not how it works here.

Ash is what remains when something refuses to disappear.

It holds shape.
It holds heat.
It holds the memory of what it used to be.

In the Archive, nothing truly burns away.

The flames are contained — carefully, deliberately — but they’re not the only thing that matters.

It’s the residue.

The way a sigil hums a fraction too long after the fire dims.
The way the air feels warmer near records that haven’t been touched in years.
The way certain shelves seem to remember hands that aren’t there anymore.

Vaelith calls it containment.

She says it like it’s a system.
Like it’s stable.
Like it can be maintained.

But if you stand there long enough, you start to notice something else.

The ash shifts.

Not visibly. Not dramatically.

Just enough that you realize it isn’t settling.

It’s waiting.

And if a flame ever returns to something that never fully let go—

it won’t start where it left off.

It will start stronger.
