Every city keeps records.

Taxes. Names. Debts. The small arithmetic of living.

Verasanth keeps something else.

Not just what you did —
but what you meant to do.
Not just where you went —
but where you hesitated.

The Warden doesn’t write everything down.
He doesn’t need to.

The ledger fills itself.

Pages turn when no one touches them.
Ink appears where there was none.
Names settle into place like they were always there, just waiting to be seen.

Some people try to stay off it.

They avoid the square.
They keep their heads down.
They leave before questions can find them.

It doesn’t matter.

The city records pressure.
Choice leaves weight.
And weight always settles somewhere.

If your name appears, you won’t be told.

You’ll notice something else instead.

Doors closing a little sooner.
Conversations ending just before you arrive.
The sense that whatever chance you had… has already been accounted for.

And if you ever find yourself standing in front of the Warden’s desk,
watching him turn a page you didn’t hear open—

don’t ask what it says.

If it concerns you,
the city has already made its decision.
