Everything has value.

That’s the first lesson.

Not everything has a price — that comes later.

The Market doesn’t deal in coins the way people expect.

You can spend Ash Marks if you want.
You can trade favors, information, whatever you think you’re holding.

But the real currency is simpler.

Attention.
Timing.
Weakness.

Stand still long enough and someone will learn something about you.

How you look at exits.
What you notice first.
What you pretend not to see.

The Market collects that.

Quietly. Efficiently.

No ledgers. No announcements.

Just patterns.

You’ll notice it the second time you come through.

A stall that wasn’t there before.
A deal offered before you asked.
A stranger who seems to know exactly what you need — and exactly what you’ll give up for it.

Lirael says the Market doesn’t hide things.

That’s true.

It just shows them to the right people.

And if you ever feel like something is being offered to you specifically—

it is.

The only question is whether you understand what it’s going to cost.
