There are fires in Verasanth that do not burn.

They remain.

The Hearthborn do not call upon flame. They do not summon it, shape it, or wield it as others might. What they carry is quieter — something that settles into the body and refuses to leave.

It does not mend what is broken.

It allows what remains to continue.

A breath steadies. A hand stops shaking. The moment that should collapse does not.

This is enough.

Those who witness it often misunderstand. They expect radiance. Renewal. A visible change that proves something has been restored.

The Hearthborn offer none of this.

What they give is harder to see.

A warmth that arrives once, and lingers longer than it should.

A strength that does not announce itself, but is simply… there when it is needed.

The Sanctum teaches that this is the truest form of flame.

Not the kind that rises.

The kind that stays.
