There are those in Verasanth who look for advantage.

The Streetcraft do not look.

They notice.

The flaw is already there.

A shift too slow. A guard held too high. The smallest hesitation, buried beneath motion, that no one else has time to understand — and no one else is positioned to use.

It does not last.

It does not announce itself.

It is simply… present.

For a moment.

The strike follows, not because it was chosen, but because there was never another outcome once the opening was seen. Clean. Precise. Gone before the response can form.

Those who survive such encounters often claim it was speed.

It was not.

Speed is movement.

This is timing.

The streets teach this early.

That weakness does not need to be forced.

It only needs to be recognized.

And that the ones who last are not the strongest.

They are the ones who see it first.

—and are no longer there when the moment closes.
