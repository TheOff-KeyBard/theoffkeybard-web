The Veil Market is not hidden.

It is misdirected.

Those who search for it directly will not find it. Those who are led to it rarely understand how they arrived. A turn taken without thought. A doorway that was not there before. A stall that seems ordinary until it is not.

This is how the Veil exists.

Not in absence.

But in distraction.

Its paths do not remain fixed. Its entrances do not remain loyal. What was open yesterday may be gone today, and what is closed now may reveal itself when it is needed.

Within, nothing is still.

Information passes from hand to hand without ever appearing to move. Deals are made in half-words and unfinished sentences. Goods change ownership before they are ever seen.

The Market does not rely on secrecy.

It relies on attention being elsewhere.

To walk the Veil is to understand this.

To run it is to decide where everyone is looking.

And more importantly…

where they are not.
