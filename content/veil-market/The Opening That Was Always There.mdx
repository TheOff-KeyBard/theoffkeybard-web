There are those in Verasanth who learn to fight.

The Streetcraft remember something else.

The moment before.

The shift in weight. The hesitation in breath. The smallest fracture in attention that no one else notices, and no one else has time to use.

They do not create opportunity.

They recognize it.

And once seen, it cannot be unseen.

Their movements are not faster.

They are earlier.

Where others react, they have already decided. Where others commit, they are already gone. The strike lands not because it was powerful, but because it was inevitable.

This is the lesson the streets teach.

That survival is not strength.

It is timing.

And that the perfect moment does not arrive.

It reveals itself.

Briefly.

To those who are looking.
