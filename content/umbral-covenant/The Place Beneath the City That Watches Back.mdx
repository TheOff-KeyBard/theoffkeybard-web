The Umbral Covenant cannot be found.

It can only be entered.

The paths that lead to it do not remain where they are left. A stairwell descends where there was none. A door opens that should not exist. A passage closes behind you, though nothing has moved.

This is not deception.

It is adjustment.

The Covenant does not hide from the city.

It exists where the city begins to change.

Below the stone, below the structure, there are places where the rules that hold Verasanth together lose their certainty. Distances lengthen. Sound arrives from the wrong direction. Shadows fall where there is no light to cast them.

The Covenant studies these places.

Not to fix them.

To understand what they are becoming.

Within its halls, nothing is entirely still. Light does not settle correctly. Reflections do not always agree. The sense of being observed does not diminish with familiarity.

Those who remain learn to ignore this.

Or to listen to it.

The Covenant does not offer safety.

It offers proximity.

To something deeper than the city itself.

Something that has not yet decided what it is.

—or what it will require.
