There are those in Verasanth who remember motion.

The Wardens remember stillness.

They do not advance unless required. They do not retreat unless forced. What they carry is not strength in the usual sense, but something heavier — a refusal to yield even when yielding would be easier.

The moment comes.

Impact. Pressure. The weight of something that should push them aside.

They remain.

This is their gift.

Not to overcome the force.

But to absorb it.

To take what would break another and hold it in place long enough for the world around them to adjust.

The Watch teaches that this is duty.

That to stand where others cannot is a promise made long before the moment arrives.

Some believe such promises are foolish.

The city has not yet learned how to move those who keep them.
