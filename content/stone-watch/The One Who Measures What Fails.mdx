Rhyla Thornshield does not ask what the city is.

She asks where it is weakening.

Where others speculate, she observes. Where others debate, she measures — distance, timing, pressure, the slow spread of something that should not be there.

Her work is not to understand Verasanth.

It is to keep it from collapsing.

She trusts neither chance nor optimism. Every patrol, every watch rotation, every reinforced gate is an answer to a question she does not speak aloud:

What happens if no one is here?

Rhyla has seen that answer.

She has no interest in seeing it again.

Those who serve under her learn quickly that protection is not an act.

It is a constant.

There is no moment where the Watch is not needed. No hour where vigilance can be set aside. The city does not sleep, and neither, in truth, do those who guard it.

She does not inspire.

She prepares.

And in Verasanth, that has proven to be far more useful.
