The Stone Watch was not built for comfort.

It was built because something had to stand where the city would not.

Set into the outer wall, its foundations merge with the same ancient stone that holds Verasanth together. It does not rise above the city so much as press against it, reinforcing what would otherwise begin to give way.

The Watch does not claim to control anything.

It claims something simpler.

That when pressure comes, there will be something there to meet it.

Inside, movement is constant. Not hurried — never that — but precise. Deliberate. Every step measured, every action aligned with something larger than the individual making it.

This is not discipline for its own sake.

It is necessity.

The Watch does not exist because the city is stable.

It exists because it is not.

There are cracks that do not show. Pressures that do not announce themselves. Failures that begin long before they are seen.

The Wardens stand where those failures would surface.

They do not stop them.

They hold them.

And for now…

that is enough.
