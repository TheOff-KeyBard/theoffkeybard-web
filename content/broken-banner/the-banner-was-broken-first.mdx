The Broken Banner did not take its name from a victory.

It took it from the moment the cloth tore and someone kept marching anyway. Garruk's people do not mythologize that instant. They file it. They measure what broke, what held, and what had to be carried in place of pride.

Discipline, in their ledgers, is not a mood. It is what remains when the body wants to stop and the city still needs a shoulder in the line.

The banner was broken first so that nothing after it could pretend it was whole.
